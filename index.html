Welcome.
This is your working document for today. You'll build four things: a map of who's already in your ecosystem, a picture of what that ecosystem can generate, a pathway showing how people move toward sustained support, and a plan for the leaders you're going to cultivate.

Go at your own pace. No wrong answers. Everything you enter is yours.

Step 1 of 4 — Relationship Asset Inventory
Who is already in your ecosystem?
Name actual people, groups, and organizations — not categories. Be specific. These are your assets.
Community & audience
The people who show up for your work. Ticket buyers, students, followers, neighbors, regulars.
Institutional & organizational
Organizations that fund, partner with, or amplify your work. Grants, fiscal sponsors, foundations, government programs, partner orgs.
Individual champions
Specific people who advocate for you personally. Advisors, mentors, collaborators, that one person who always shows up and brings others.
Platform & in-kind
Non-cash resources you have access to. A venue that gives you space, a friend who designs your flyers, a podcast that features you, equipment you borrow.

EncounterSomeone sees your work for the first time. A show, an Instagram post, a friend’s recommendation, a flyer. Where does first contact actually happen in your community?
EngageThey come back or take action — buy a ticket, take a class, share your post, show up to something. What does engagement look like specifically in your ecosystem?
GiveThey contribute something — money, labor, platform, time. What makes someone in your community give in the moment? What does that ask look like?
SustainThey keep showing up and bringing others. What has actually converted someone from occasional to consistent in your world? Name the thing.

Step 4 of 4 — Community Leader Cultivation
